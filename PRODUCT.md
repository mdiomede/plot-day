# Plot Day

## Product purpose
A free daily garden-optimization puzzle ("Wordle for gardeners"). Everyone gets the same procedurally generated yard each day: obstacles cast moving shadows, a seed packet and water budget force choices, and the result is a score plus a shareable picture of the garden you grew. Sessions are bounded (5 to 15 minutes) by design; the game never asks for more time than it earns.

## Register
product

## Users
Casual daily-puzzle players (Wordle, TimeGuessr crowd) who want a short, satisfying think with their morning coffee, plus gardeners who get an extra layer of recognition from the theme. No accounts, no monetization. Competition is asynchronous: same board, compare scores.

## Tone
Cozy, warm, storybook. A tiny garden journal, not a SaaS dashboard. Friendly but never childish; the puzzle underneath is sharp and full-information, like chess in a sundress.

## Strategic principles
- Zero rules to learn on day one: sun-lovers vs shade-lovers is common knowledge; everything else is visible before you commit.
- Full information, deterministic outcome. No dice, no hidden synergies.
- The share artifact is a pretty garden. Any mechanic that fills gardens with wilted spam is wrong.
- One developer must be able to run this forever: content comes from a seeded generator, not authored levels.

## Anti-references
- Pokemon GO style time/money extraction. Nothing in this game nags, gates, or monetizes.
- Generic puzzle-app chrome (gray grids, system fonts, modal tutorials).
- Wordle clone aesthetics (dark tiles, keyboard rows).

## Current stage
Playable mechanics prototype (static HTML/CSS/JS, emoji art). Rules still being balance-tuned through play. A full illustrated art pass is planned once rules stabilize; until then, UI polish should maximize legibility and charm within the emoji-placeholder constraint.
